Visual representation

Generate good looking image/movie with VMD

The objective of this page is to give a few tips to improve the representation of molecular system using VMD. The examples that are showed have been made using the version 1.9.4a51 of VMD.

If you are new to LAMMPS, this website is for you, see for example this tutorial for beginners. Please feel free to contact me by email at simon.gravelle at live.fr, I always answer to molecular dynamics questions.




Practical example

If you want to test the tips, you can download this LAMMPS trajectory file. The trajectory file corresponds to a mixture between water and toluene, and can be openned with VMD by typing in a terminal: 

vmd dump.lammpstrj




The representation

You can change the representation of the atoms by going in Graphics, Representations, and changing the Drawing Method. My favorite representations are dynamic bonds for large molecules or graphene, and VDW for single atoms or small molecules like water. You can select to apply a representation only to a group of atoms, by choosing a group in the windows Selected Atoms. To obtain the same result as me, replace All by type 1 in the windows to select the oxygen of the water molecules, tune the radius (0.8), and the resolution (>32 at least). Then, click on Create Rep to create a second representation for the hydrogen of water (type 2), and change the radius to 0.4. Create a third representation for the toluene, enter type 3 4 5 to select the carbon, the oxygen, and the hydrogen atoms of toluene. Choose DynamicBonds and increase the resolution. 'DynamicBonds' is not really good looking by itself. To smooth the representation, let us create the fourth and last representation (VDW, radius 0.2) for types 3, 4, and 5.




The colours

To change the colours, go to Graphics, Colors. Click on Display, then Background, and choose white instead of black. Then click on Name, and 5 (this is the oxygen atoms), and choose red. Do the same for 4 (carbon, pink), 3 (hydrogen, white), 2 (hydrogen, white). These three colours are standar for oxygen, carbon, and hydrogen. To mark the difference between the water and the toluene, let us choose a different colour for 1. Enter manually the values 0 0.6, 0.88 in the RGB boxes.




The materials

An important parameter is the texture of the material, and finding the right degree of shininess is often difficult. In the Representations windows, you can choose among several materials. You can also create your own. Go in Graphics, Materials. Click on Opaque (the one used by default), then Create New. Hit Create New again. Two new materials should have appeared, called Material23 and Material24. Rename them to MWater and MToluene. For MWater, change Diffuse, Specular, and Shininess, to 0.43, 0.48, and 0.35, respectively. Then, from the Representations windows, change the Material from Opaque to MWater. Do the same for toluene, with values of 0.78, 0.33, and 0.32 for MToluene.




The view

Finally we are going to change the view. Go in Display, and click Orthographic. You can zoom with the mouse wheel. The system is ready to be rendered in high resolution.




Rendering

Image

To generate high resolution image, go in File, Render. Choose Tachyon, add -res 1000 1000 in the Render command cell, and hit Start Rendering.

Movie

To generate high resolution movie, go in Extension, Vizualisation, and Movie Maker. If you hit Make Movie directly, the movie generated by VMD will be of poor quality. Instead, we are going to generate a sequence of image, and assemble these images ourselves. Go in Movie Settings, hit Trajectory, and uncheck Delete image files. In Rendered, choose Tachyon, then Make Movie. When its done, you can close VMD. If you don't want do redo these steps every time, you can save the VMD state by clicking File and Save vizualisation state. From the terminal, assemble the image into a movie by typing: 

ffmpeg -r 60 -i untitled.%05d.ppm -vcodec libx264 -crf 0  -pix_fmt yuv420p myvideo.mp4
You may receive the following error width not divisible by 2 (1363x1134). If that's the case, you can remove one line of pixel with the command: 
for file in untitled.*.ppm; do convert $file -crop 1362x1134+0+0 output-$file; done
The result should look like this video.
